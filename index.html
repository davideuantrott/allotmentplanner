Seed Starting Tips

Brassicas (Cauliflower & PSB)

  • Thin to the strongest seedling per cell — use scissors, don’t pull.
  • Firm soil very well after transplanting (critical on clay).
  • Net Bed 3 immediately after planting — pigeons will destroy unprotected brassicas overnight.

Tomatoes

  • Turn trays 180° daily to prevent leggy growth.
  • Keep in warmest spot (18–21°C) until germinated, then bright windowsill.
  • Feed weekly with half-strength tomato feed after potting on.
  • Hardening off is essential — never skip this step!

Lettuce

  • Surface sow — needs light to germinate (do not cover).
  • Quick grower: only ~4 weeks from sow to transplant.
  • After March batches: switch to direct sowing in bed every 2 weeks.

Leeks

  • Root trainers are best for clay soil — deep roots establish better.
  • Can trim tops to 8cm if plants get leggy indoors.
  • Water at depth when transplanting, not just the surface.
  • Drop into dibber hole and water in — no need to fill hole with soil.

Growing Advice for No-Dig on Heavy Clay

2026 Season Adjustments

  • Leeks: 3-batch succession (Batch 2 missed 23 Feb). Impact: potential 2–3 week gap late Nov. Mitigation: leave Batch 1 in ground until Batch 3 ready. ~62 leeks (3/week) — still adequate.
  • Tomatoes: Over-seeded 72 seeds vs planned 30 — excellent selection opportunity. Thin during potting → select 20 in late April → choose best 7–8 mid-May. Gift ~12 quality plants!
  • Deep root trainers vs standard propagator: Comparing Batch 1 (standard) vs Batches 3 & 4 (deep trainer). Hypothesis: deep roots establish faster on clay.

Seed Quantities Reference

  • Leeks: 24–26 seeds, 12–13 cells, 2/cell → 20–22 plants
  • Tomatoes: 10 = adequate, 24 = excellent selection (over-seed by 2×!)
  • Lettuce (indoor): 8 seeds, 8 cells, 1/cell → 6–7 plants
  • Cauliflower: 12 seeds, 6 cells, 2/cell → 5–6 plants
  • PSB: 8 seeds, 4 cells, 2/cell → 3–4 plants
  • Chard: 10 seeds, 5 cells, 2/cell → 4–5 plants (clusters! thin TWICE)
  • Nasturtiums: 16 seeds, 8 × 9cm pots (use pots, not cells!)
  • Basil: 10–12 seeds, 4–5 pots, needs 18–20°C

Thinning Best Practices

  • When: First true leaves visible (not seed leaves). Seedlings 2–3cm tall.
  • Chard exception: Thin TWICE — to 2/cell, then to 1/cell.
  • How: Clean scissors, snip at soil level — NEVER pull! Pulling disturbs remaining seedling’s roots.
  • Choose: Tallest, thickest stem, darkest green, most leaves.
  • Water gently after thinning to settle remaining seedling.

Clay Soil Firming Guide

  • FIRM VERY WELL — Brassicas only: Cauliflower and PSB. Use heel of boot, press HARD from all directions. Plant must NOT rock. Wind rock = poor roots = no heads.
  • PSB needs EXTRA firm: Grows 90cm+ tall, faces winter winds. Even firmer than cauliflower. Check and re-firm weekly for first month.
  • DON’T over-firm: Leeks, lettuce, chard, tomatoes. Water-in creates soil contact. Clay holds other crops naturally.

Windowsill Space Management

  • Peak occupancy: Late March–April (~80 cells + pots).
  • Temperature zones: Warmest (18–21°C) for tomatoes and basil. Moderate (12–18°C) for lettuce, chard. Coolest (10–15°C) for leeks and brassicas.
  • Turn ALL trays/pots 180° daily to prevent leaning toward light.
  • Space saving: Transplant early batches outdoors to clear space. Stagger sowings. Move plants outside during hardening phase.

No-Dig Foundations

  • Never walk on your beds — all work from the paths.
  • Layer 15cm of good compost over cardboard each season.
  • For root crops (carrots), pierce the cardboard with a crowbar (max 2cm) and fill with compost + sharp sand.
  • For leeks, use a dibber to make deep holes through the cardboard.
  • For brassicas only: firm the soil before planting (the one compaction exception).

Clay Soil Adjustments

  • Clay stays cold and wet longer — delay direct sowing by 1–2 weeks vs lighter soils.
  • Start crops indoors on windowsills where possible for a head start.
  • Raised beds and compost insulate against clay's worst effects.
  • Avoid working soil when wet — no-dig largely solves this.
  • Add sharp sand to carrot planting holes to prevent forking.

Succession Planting Strategy

  • Lettuce: Small pinch every 2 weeks (12 sowings total).
  • Radish: Short row every 3 weeks. Harvest 25 days after sowing.
  • Carrots: 60cm row every 3–4 weeks from April.
  • Beetroot: 50cm row every 3 weeks from April.
  • Spinach: Every 3 weeks Mar–May, restart late Aug. Avoid July.
  • No succession needed: tomatoes, potatoes, garlic, onions, shallots, cauliflower, PSB, chard, strawberries, basil.

Companion Planting Logic

  • Nasturtiums are your primary trap crop — aphids prefer them over veg. Every bed corner.
  • Marigolds deter carrot fly and whitefly. Dot between carrots, tomatoes, strawberries.
  • Basil + Tomatoes — classic companions. May help deter whitefly.
  • Spinach + Potatoes — good neighbours, no competition.
  • Lettuce/Spinach inter-crop with brassicas — quick harvest before brassicas fill out.

Potato Chitting & Planting

  • Place seed potatoes in egg boxes, rose end (most eyes) facing UP.
  • Cool, bright room — NOT warm. Short, sturdy green/purple sprouts wanted.
  • Chit for 4–6 weeks before planting date.
  • Plant 1st Earlies first (late March), 2nd Earlies (early April), Maincrop (late April).
  • Earth up with compost and straw as shoots emerge.

Space Re-Use After Harvesting

  • After garlic/onions (Jul): Transplant leeks into vacated space.
  • After 1st Early potatoes (Jun): Sow lettuce or spinach.
  • After all potatoes (Aug/Sep): Late spinach, or compost for winter.
  • Between young brassicas (May/Jun): Inter-crop lettuce & spinach.
  • Around young tomatoes (May/Jun): Radish at base — harvest before tomatoes fill out.

Hardening Off

  • All indoor-raised seedlings must be hardened off before transplanting.
  • Place outside in shelter for increasing periods over 7–10 days.
  • Start with 2 hours, increase daily. Bring in at night at first.
  • Critical for: tomatoes, cauliflower, PSB, chard, nasturtiums, marigolds, basil.
  • Not needed for: sets (onions, shallots), seed potatoes, strawberry plants.

  • 9 Mar: Plant onion AND shallot sets.

  • Weekly from Jun: Tomato feed once first truss appears.
